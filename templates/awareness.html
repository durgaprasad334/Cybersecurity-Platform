Protecting Data, Identity & Digital Life

What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, servers, mobile devices, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, damage, or theft. It ensures confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.

Why is Cybersecurity Important?

  • Protects personal and financial information
  • Prevents identity theft and fraud
  • Secures business and government systems
  • Maintains trust in digital platforms
  • Prevents data loss and service downtime

How to Protect Your Data

  • Always keep software and operating systems updated
  • Use antivirus and firewall protection
  • Backup important data regularly
  • Encrypt sensitive files and drives
  • Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions

Password Protection Best Practices

  • Use strong passwords (uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols)
  • Minimum password length: 12 characters
  • Never reuse the same password
  • Use a password manager
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)

Bad Cyber Practices (Do NOT Do This)

  • Clicking unknown or suspicious links
  • Sharing OTPs or passwords
  • Using weak passwords like 123456
  • Installing cracked or pirated software
  • Ignoring security warnings

Phishing & Social Engineering

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information by pretending to be trusted entities via emails, messages, or fake websites.

  • Always verify sender email addresses
  • Check website URLs carefully
  • Do not click urgent or threatening messages
  • Never share credentials through email or SMS

Losses Due to Cyber Attacks

  • Financial loss and bank fraud
  • Loss of personal identity
  • Business reputation damage
  • Data leakage and privacy violations
  • Legal consequences and penalties
